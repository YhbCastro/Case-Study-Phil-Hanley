Role: Lead UX Design/Front-end Engineer

Duration: 2 weeks

Platform: Squarespace

Website update

Make the website mobile-friendly: Ensure the site is fully responsive and functions well on all mobile devices.

Improve the UX/UI: Enhance the user experience and interface design for better usability and visual appeal.

Remove unnecessary information: Streamline content by eliminating irrelevant or redundant information.

Facilitate future changes: Implement a design that allows the client to make updates easily without compromising the overall design.

The client reached out requesting to make their website responsive and mobile-friendly. During our initial interaction, I gathered additional information about the future changes they would like to implement to further enhance the user experience.

Goals
Overview
Previous Website

The main initial issues were:

The header image did not fit the cree properly.

The menu buttons were too small to be clicked on a phone screen.

The text color contrast made it difficult to read.

My initial design

My initial design changes include:

Fixing the image fitting issues

Moving the menu above the picture to resolve contrast problems.

Removing the social media links from the menu and placing them as icons after the image for a cleaner visual layout.

Client take and actual design

The client approved the design with a few minor changes:

Remove unnecessary and repeated elements, such as social media links appearing multiple times throughout the website.

Moved the social media icons to the bottom of the page.

Limited the menu to only inlude links to external pages.

Made the "Join the Email List" section the main point of interest on the page.

Final design

A week later, the client requested some final changes to the website to promote the release of his book. After these updates, I ensured the design was adjusted so he could make future changes himself without worrying about breaking the CSS.
